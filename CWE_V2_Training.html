System Overview

What is the Claims Workflow Engine?

A Google Sheets-based claims management system that guides billing staff through every step of claim resolution via a persistent sidebar dashboard.

🎯 Purpose

The Claims Workflow Engine V2.5 (CWE) standardizes how your organization handles denied, rejected, underpaid, and internally flagged claims. It replaces ad-hoc spreadsheet tracking with a structured, auditable process that every team member follows the same way — accessed from a sidebar that opens automatically when you open the spreadsheet.

🗂️ How It's Structured

  • Sidebar Dashboard — Your primary working area. Opens automatically. Shows your queue, metrics, filters, and claim workflow view.
  • CWE App (Canvas) — The background sheet showing live team-wide metrics: open claims, critical/high count, escalated count, and stage breakdown.
  • Claims Sheet — Backend data store. Billers never work here directly — all actions happen through the sidebar.
  • Activity Log — Every action, note, and edit is recorded here automatically with timestamp and user name.
  • REF Sheets — Reference data maintained by supervisors: CARC codes, MACs, Payers, Practices, Providers, Global Periods, Denial Categories, TPL Carriers.

🔄 Claim Lifecycle

Every claim moves through defined stages. The system guides valid transitions and records every change.

NEW→ WORKING→ APPEALED→ ESCALATED→ RESOLVED or CLOSED

Claims can also move to PENDING INFO when waiting on documentation, or back to WORKING from any active stage.

👥 User Roles

  • Analyst / Biller — Sees only claims assigned to their group. Works their personal queue. Cannot close claims or view other billers' queues.
  • Supervisor — Sees all claims across all groups. Uses "Viewing As" to see any biller's exact perspective. Can reassign and close claims.
  • Admin — Full access. Has additional tools including the Test Claim button for system validation.

User Manual

Logging Claims

How to use the intake form to log a Denial, Rejection, Payment Issue, or Internal Issue.

Click + Log New Issue on the dashboard. A modal form opens. Select your issue type first — the fields change based on what you select.

🚫 Denial

The payer received, processed, and refused the claim. Always has a CARC code on the EOB. Use for CO, PR, and OA denial codes. Appeal deadlines apply.

❌ Rejection

The claim was never processed — returned by clearinghouse or payer due to a technical or formatting error. Must be corrected and resubmitted. No appeal deadline since the claim was never adjudicated.

💰 Payment Issue

The claim was paid but the amount is incorrect — underpayment, overpayment, or contractual dispute. Enter billed and paid amounts; variance calculates automatically.

⚙️ Internal Issue

An internal workflow or system problem — batch errors, duplicate policies, wrong clearinghouse routing, missing NPI in batch. Not payer-facing.

Key Field Notes

Account # vs PTID

The Account Number is the unique encounter/bill ID from your billing software — it never changes for a given encounter. The PTID (Patient ID) can be used instead when the account number isn't immediately available. At least one is required for all non-internal claims.

CARC or Denial Category

For Denial claims, enter either the CARC code (searched from the reference table) or select a Denial Category. At least one is required. When a CARC code is selected, the Denial Category auto-fills. You can override it if needed.

💡
After you submit, the form closes and the new claim opens automatically in the workflow view — ready to start working immediately.
⚠️
Each account number should only appear once as an open claim. If you log a claim with an account number already open in the system, it will alert you and redirect to the existing claim.

Working Claims

How to use the workflow view to advance, document, escalate, and resolve claims.

Click any claim in your queue. The workflow view loads. Use ← Back in the top-left to return to the dashboard.

📌 Claim Header

Shows the Issue ID, payer, account number, issue type, and stage. The colored type badge (red=Denial, orange=Rejection, etc.) lets you instantly identify what you're working.

📊 Payer Intelligence

Shows timely filing deadline and days remaining, plus Level 1 and Level 2 appeal windows. Color-coded: green = safe, yellow = under 30 days, red = under 15 days.

🏥 MAC Information (Medicare only)

Shows the Part B and DME MAC for the practice state — jurisdiction code and contact number. Appears automatically for Medicare claims.

🔀 Workflow Guide

A decision tree based on the claim's denial type. Walks you through the steps specific to this kind of denial. Follow the prompts — they're built from established billing protocols.

▶️ Start Working

Advances the claim from NEW to WORKING. Always click this before taking any action on the claim. It establishes your audit trail.

✏️ Edit Claim Details

Opens the edit form pre-populated with all current claim data. Use to correct intake errors, update payer, or add missing fields. All changes are recorded field-by-field in the Activity Log.

📝 Log Activity / Notes

Document everything you do: who you called, reference numbers, what was corrected, next steps. Every note is timestamped and permanently stored in the Activity Log.

📋
Golden rule: Always add a note before advancing a stage. A claim with no notes is a claim that can't be defended in an audit or explained to a payer. If you worked it, document it.

CARC Codes & Denial Categories

How denial codes drive the system's guidance — and how to use them correctly at intake.

📖 What is a CARC?

A Claim Adjustment Reason Code (CARC) is a standardized code payers attach to remittance advice (EOB/ERA) to explain why a claim was adjusted or denied. The group code prefix tells you who is financially responsible:

  • CO (Contractual Obligation) — Payer adjustment per contract. Provider typically cannot bill patient.
  • PR (Patient Responsibility) — Patient owes this (deductible, copay, coinsurance).
  • OA (Other Adjustment) — Other reason, varies by context.
  • PI (Payer Initiated) — Payer adjustment not related to contract or patient responsibility.

Entering a CARC at Intake

Type in the CARC field
As you type, the system searches your REF-CARC table and shows matching codes with descriptions. Search by code number or description keyword.
Select the matching code
Click the matching result. The Denial Category field auto-fills. You can override if the auto-fill doesn't match your situation.
Workflow guide populates
Once logged, the workflow view shows a decision tree specific to this denial type — guiding you through the correct resolution steps for that CARC.
⚠️
CARC actions in REF-CARC are maintained by your supervisor. If guidance is missing or incorrect for a code, notify your supervisor. Do not edit the reference sheet directly.

MAC Lookup

How the system finds your Medicare Administrative Contractor automatically.

🔍
MAC information only appears in the workflow view when the claim's payer contains "Medicare." For all other payers, this section stays hidden.

What is a MAC?

Medicare Administrative Contractors (MACs) are private companies contracted by CMS to process Medicare claims. Two types matter for billing:

  • Part B MAC — Processes outpatient professional services. Contact for claim status, reopenings, and redeterminations.
  • DME MAC — Processes durable medical equipment claims only.

Each MAC covers specific states and is identified by a Jurisdiction code. The system automatically displays the correct MAC based on the practice state — no manual lookup needed.

How to Use MAC Information

Open a Medicare claim
Click the claim in your queue. The MAC card appears automatically in the workflow view.
Note the MAC name and jurisdiction code
You'll see the Part B MAC name, jurisdiction code, and contact number. Use the jurisdiction code when referencing the claim with the MAC.
Call the MAC provider line
Have your NPI, patient Medicare ID, and claim DOS ready before calling. Log the reference number from the call in the claim notes.

Global Periods

Understanding surgical global periods and why they affect claim billing and denials.

📅 What is a Global Period?

A global period is a defined window of time following a surgical procedure during which Medicare — and most commercial payers — consider all related services to be bundled into the original surgery payment. This means the surgeon's pre-op visit, the surgery itself, and all routine post-operative care within the global window are all paid for by that single surgery payment.

Global periods are either 0 days (endoscopies and minor procedures), 10 days (minor surgery), or 90 days (major surgery).

⚠️ Why This Causes Denials

If a provider bills a follow-up E&M visit or another service during the global period of a previous surgery without the correct modifier, the payer will deny it — typically with CARC CO-97 (bundled service) or CO-88. Common scenarios:

  • Patient has knee replacement (CPT 27447, 90-day global). Provider bills 99213 follow-up 3 weeks later → Denied as bundled.
  • Provider bills the same surgical CPT twice for the same patient within the global period.
  • A separate, unrelated problem is addressed during the global period but not properly documented or modified.

🔑 Modifiers That Override Bundling

When a service is legitimately separate from the surgical package, specific modifiers tell the payer to process it separately:

  • Modifier -24 — Unrelated E&M visit during post-op global period
  • Modifier -25 — Significant, separately identifiable E&M on same day as procedure
  • Modifier -58 — Staged or related procedure during post-op period
  • Modifier -78 — Unplanned return to OR during post-op period
  • Modifier -79 — Unrelated procedure during post-op period

💡 How CWE Uses Global Period Data

When a biller enters a CPT code at intake, the system checks the REF-GlobalPeriods table. If the code has a global period, a yellow warning appears: "⚠️ Global Period Alert: CPT [X] carries a 90-day global period. When was the last time this code was billed for this patient?" This prompts you to check whether a recent surgery might explain the denial before logging with incorrect information.

📋
Always check global period status before resubmitting a CO-97 denial. Resubmitting without the correct modifier will result in another denial and burns more of your timely filing window.

MassHealth TPL Carrier Codes

Understanding Third Party Liability and why it causes denials on MassHealth claims.

🔀 What is TPL?

Third Party Liability (TPL) refers to the legal obligation of a third party — typically a private health insurer, workers' compensation carrier, or auto insurer — to pay for healthcare services that MassHealth (Massachusetts Medicaid) would otherwise cover. Under federal law, Medicaid is always the payer of last resort. If a MassHealth member also has private insurance, that private insurance must be billed first. MassHealth only pays what remains after the other insurance pays.

📋 What are TPL Carrier Codes?

MassHealth assigns a unique TPL Carrier Code to every private insurance company it tracks as a potential third-party payer. These codes:

  • Identify the specific insurance carrier in MassHealth's system
  • Are required on claims when a member has other insurance on file
  • Allow MassHealth to coordinate benefits automatically
  • Help prevent billing fraud by ensuring private insurance is always billed first

⚠️ Why Denials Happen

  • Missing TPL code — MassHealth has other insurance on record but the claim didn't include it. Denial: "Other insurance on file."
  • Wrong TPL code — The carrier code doesn't match MassHealth's records for this member.
  • COB not completed — Primary insurance hasn't been billed yet, or the EOB wasn't attached.
  • Outdated TPL info — Member's other insurance changed but MassHealth records weren't updated.

✅ Resolution Steps

  1. Verify the patient's current insurance with the patient and in your PM system
  2. Look up the correct TPL carrier code using the reference table
  3. Ensure primary insurance was billed first — have the EOB ready
  4. If member has no other insurance, contact MassHealth to update their records
  5. Resubmit with the correct carrier code and primary EOB attached
💡
Always verify TPL status at every visit. A patient's other insurance can change at any time — an outdated TPL record will cause every claim to deny until it's corrected in MassHealth's system.
Role Tutorial

Biller Workflow

👤 Biller / Analyst   Your daily process for working the claims queue.

Daily Startup

Open the spreadsheet
The CWE sidebar opens automatically. No menu navigation. Your dashboard shows today's queue, metrics, and priority breakdown immediately.
Review your metrics
Check Total Open, Critical/High count, and Escalated. The By Priority breakdown tells you what needs attention today before you even open a claim.
Work your queue in priority order
The queue is pre-sorted: Critical first, then High, then Medium, then oldest-first within each priority. Work top to bottom — don't cherry-pick.

Working a Claim (Each Claim)

Click the claim
The workflow view loads. First thing: check the timely filing days remaining in the Payer Intelligence card. If under 30 days, treat as Critical regardless of assigned priority.
Click Start Working
Always do this before any action. It advances the claim to WORKING and creates your audit trail.
Follow the workflow guide
Read the decision tree for this denial type. It tells you exactly what to check, who to call, and what to do. Don't skip steps.
Take action in your billing software
Make corrections, resubmit, or submit an appeal — whatever the guide directs. Keep the sidebar open while you work.
Log your activity
Return to the sidebar and document what you did: corrections made, who you spoke to, confirmation numbers, next expected step.
Advance the stage
Move to APPEALED after submitting an appeal, PENDING INFO if waiting on records, ESCALATED if you need supervisor help. Never leave a claim in WORKING with no note and no stage change at end of day.

When to Escalate

  • Claim is over $500 and has been denied twice with no clear resolution path
  • Claim is within 15 days of timely filing deadline and you cannot resolve it today
  • Workflow guide says "Supervisor review required"
  • You believe the payer made an error but need authority to pursue a formal appeal
  • A patient or provider has contacted the office directly about this claim
  • You are unsure which action to take — when in doubt, escalate with a note

Supervisor Workflow

👑 Supervisor   Managing the team queue, handling escalations, and monitoring team metrics.

Using "Viewing As"

Open the dashboard
The gold "Viewing: My Queue" bar appears below the Log New Issue button. This is your supervisor control.
Switch to a team member's view

Work or review their claims
Open any claim in their queue, reassign it, or take action directly. This is your primary coaching and oversight tool.

Daily Supervisor Tasks

  • Check your dashboard for the escalated claim count — resolve or reassign within 24 hours
  • Use "Viewing as" to spot-check each biller's queue — look for claims aging without notes
  • Review the CWE App canvas sheet for team-wide metrics and stage distribution
  • Check for claims approaching timely filing deadlines across the full team
  • Approve or deny write-off requests from billers for CLOSED stage transitions

Maintaining Reference Data

Updating CARC Actions (REF-CARC)

Find the CARC code row and update the Suggested Action column. Changes take effect immediately for all new claims loaded after the update.

Adding Practices / Providers

Add new rows to REF-Practices and REF-Providers. New entries appear in the intake form dropdowns on the next load.

Adding Payers

Add to REF-Payers with state, coverage type, plan type, timely filing window, and appeal levels. This populates the Payer Intelligence card in the workflow view for all billers.

⚠️
When adding or editing REF data, be careful with column order. The system reads columns by index — inserting a column in the wrong position can break form lookups across all billers.

Global Period Reference

Common CPT codes and their global period lengths. Used by CWE to alert billers at intake.

ℹ️
Global periods are defined by CMS and apply to Medicare. Most commercial payers follow the same rules. When in doubt, verify with the specific payer.

Global Period Types

  • 0 days — Minor procedures, endoscopies. Only the day-of service is bundled. Follow-up visits the next day are billable.
  • 10 days — Minor surgery. Same-day pre-op, surgery, and 10 days of routine post-op are all included in the global payment.
  • 90 days — Major surgery. Pre-op 1 day before, surgery, and 90 days of routine post-op are all included.
  • MMM — Maternity care. Global OB package covers all routine prenatal, delivery, and post-partum care.
  • XXX — Global period concept does not apply to this procedure.

MassHealth TPL Carrier Reference

Third Party Liability carrier codes for MassHealth claims. Full list maintained in REF-TPL sheet.

What is Third Party Liability?

Under federal Medicaid law, MassHealth is always the payer of last resort. If a MassHealth member has other insurance — commercial, Medicare, workers' comp, or auto — that insurance must be billed first. MassHealth only pays what remains. TPL carrier codes identify those other insurers within MassHealth's system, and must appear on claims when other insurance is on file.

Support

Help & FAQ

Answers to common questions about using the Claims Workflow Engine.

The sidebar didn't open when I opened the spreadsheet. ▼
The sidebar opens automatically on spreadsheet load. If it didn't appear, go to Extensions → Claims Workflow → Open Dashboard. If that menu isn't visible, refresh the page and wait 10–15 seconds for Apps Script to initialize. If the problem persists, contact your supervisor.
I logged a claim with the wrong issue type. Can I fix it? ▼
Yes. Use the Edit Claim Details button in the workflow view to correct the issue type. The change will be recorded field-by-field in the Activity Log. If the claim is already in an advanced stage, notify your supervisor before editing so they're aware.
Why can't I see claims assigned to other billers? ▼
Billers only see claims assigned to their own group — this is by design. It keeps your queue focused on your work. Supervisors can see all claims and can use "Viewing As" to see any individual biller's exact perspective.
A resolved claim needs to be reworked. Can it be reopened? ▼
Yes, but only by a supervisor. Contact your supervisor and explain why the claim needs to be reworked (e.g., payer recouped payment, new denial received for the same account number). The supervisor will move it back to WORKING with a note.
When I log a new claim, who does it get assigned to? ▼
By default, new claims are assigned to the person who logs them. If the Assign To field is left blank at intake, the system assigns it to you. Supervisors can reassign any claim at any time from the workflow view.
I searched for a CARC code and it's not showing up in the system. ▼
If a CARC code isn't in the search results, you can type it in manually — the field accepts free text. Notify your supervisor so they can add the code to the REF-CARC sheet with the proper description and suggested action for future use.
I tried to log a claim and got an alert that the account number already exists. ▼
Each account number should appear only once as an open claim. The system will redirect you to the existing open claim. If the existing claim is assigned to another biller, contact your supervisor — they can assess whether a duplicate entry is truly needed or whether the existing claim should be reassigned.
A patient has the same denial across 5 different account numbers. Log them all separately? ▼
Log the primary claim normally. In the notes at intake, list the related account numbers and describe the pattern. This keeps your queue manageable while documenting all affected accounts. When resolved, apply the same fix to the related accounts and note them all as resolved.
Where can I see the full history of everything done on a claim? ▼
The Activity Log sheet records every action: claim logged, stage changes, notes added, field-level edits (what changed from what to what), and reassignments — all with timestamps and user names. Supervisors can also access this for auditing and coaching purposes.
The canvas metrics seem out of date. How do I update them? ▼
The CWE App canvas updates automatically when new claims are logged. For a manual refresh, click the Refresh button on the dashboard. If metrics still appear stale, go to Extensions → Claims Workflow → Refresh Metrics for a full rebuild.
A yellow global period warning appeared when I entered a CPT code. What does it mean? ▼
The system found that this CPT code has a global period defined in REF-GlobalPeriods. It's prompting you to verify whether this claim might be related to a recent surgery — if so, the denial may be because the follow-up visit is bundled into the global payment. Check the patient's surgical history before proceeding.
MassHealth denied for "other insurance on file" but the patient says they don't have other insurance. ▼
MassHealth's records may be outdated. Contact MassHealth's TPL department to update the member's insurance record. You'll need to provide proof that the other insurance is no longer active (termination letter, expired insurance card, etc.). Once MassHealth updates their records, resubmit the claim.
The "Polish with AI" button in the resolution form doesn't seem to be working. ▼
The AI Polish feature requires an Anthropic API key configured in the system's Script Properties. If the button produces no result or shows an error, the key may not be set up yet. Contact your system administrator or supervisor to configure it.

Troubleshooting

Common technical issues and how to resolve them.

🔄
Sidebar shows a loading spinner and never loads
Close and reopen the sidebar via Extensions → Claims Workflow → Open Dashboard. If it persists, refresh the entire Google Sheet. Apps Script occasionally times out on initial load, especially after the sheet has been inactive.
📋
Log New Issue form won't submit — shows red error messages
Red-bordered fields are required fields that are empty. Fill in all highlighted fields. The error summary at the top of the form lists exactly what's missing. Check that Account Number OR PTID is filled (at least one is required for non-internal claims).
🔍
Payer dropdown is empty after selecting a practice
The payer list filters by the practice's configured state. If no payers appear, the selected practice may not have a state set in REF-Practices. Contact your supervisor to verify the practice's REF entry is complete.
👁️
My queue shows claims that don't belong to me
Check the "Viewing As" bar — it may still be set to another biller's view from a previous supervisor session. Select "My Queue" from the dropdown to reset to your personal view.
✏️
Edit Claim form fields are blank instead of pre-filled
The form prefills asynchronously — some dropdowns take 2–3 seconds to populate after opening. Wait briefly before assuming fields are broken. If still blank after 5 seconds, the original data may have been saved with missing values. Re-enter manually and save to correct the record.
📊
Dashboard metrics show different numbers than what I expect
Click Refresh on the dashboard — the sidebar pulls data on load and on manual refresh, not in real time. If counts still don't match after refreshing, contact your supervisor. There may be data that was edited outside the sidebar.
💾
Mark Resolved fails with an error after clicking Confirm
Ensure all three resolution fields are filled: Resolution Action, Outcome, and Resolution Notes. The form will not submit with any of these empty. If the error persists after filling all fields, try refreshing the sidebar and reopening the claim.
🏥
MAC information isn't showing on a Medicare claim
The MAC card only displays when the payer name contains "Medicare." If the payer was entered with a different name (e.g., "CMS" or "Medicare Advantage"), the MAC lookup won't trigger. Edit the claim to correct the payer name, or look up the MAC manually in the MAC Reference page.
🔒
"You do not have permission" error when opening a claim
The claim is assigned to a group you don't have access to. This can happen if you received a shared link to a specific claim. Return to your dashboard — you'll only see claims within your assigned group. If you believe the claim should be in your group, contact your supervisor.
💡
If you encounter an issue not listed here, use the Log Activity field to note what happened, then contact your supervisor. Include: the claim ID, the action you were trying to take, and the exact error message if one appeared.
